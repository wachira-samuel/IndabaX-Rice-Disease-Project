Causes

Rice leaf blast is caused by the fungus Magnaporthe oryzae. It thrives in warm, humid environments and can spread rapidly under favorable conditions.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Lesions on leaves that are diamond-shaped and have a gray center.
  • Leaf tips may wither and die.
  • In severe cases, the entire leaf can be affected, leading to significant yield loss.

Treatment

Effective management includes the use of resistant rice varieties, proper field sanitation, and fungicide application when necessary.

Recommendations

  • Rotate crops to prevent the buildup of the fungus in the soil.
  • Ensure proper drainage to reduce humidity levels in the field.
  • Monitor fields regularly for early detection and management.
